# Initial Findings

## Research Objective

Identify common patterns used by successful B2B SaaS creators on LinkedIn.

---

## Finding 1: Education Comes Before Promotion

Across Justin Welsh, Amanda Natividad, and Chris Walker, the majority of content focuses on educating the audience rather than promoting products.

Implication:
B2B SaaS brands should prioritize teaching before selling.

---

## Finding 2: Strong Hooks Drive Engagement

The first sentence is often designed to create curiosity, surprise, or challenge conventional thinking.

Implication:
Every LinkedIn post should begin with a strong hook.

---

## Finding 3: One Post, One Idea

High-performing content usually focuses on a single concept.

Implication:
Avoid combining multiple messages into one post.

---

## Finding 4: Personal Experience Builds Credibility

Creators frequently use personal stories and lessons learned.

Implication:
Founders and operators should share real experiences instead of generic advice.

---

## Finding 5: Audience Trust Is Built Before Conversion

The creators focus on building trust and attention before asking for any action.

Implication:
Audience growth should be treated as a long-term asset.
